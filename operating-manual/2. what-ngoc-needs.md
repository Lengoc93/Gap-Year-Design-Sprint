## Core Values (in compass order)

### 1. Sovereignty (root, 9.0)

**What it means in practice:** Control over daily structure, decisions, and identity. Externally-imposed rigid schedules = anxiety activation. Not "doing whatever I want" — it's "choosing what I commit to from my own authority."

**I know sovereignty is present when:** I feel calm, clear, and decisive. My body is relaxed. I can say no without guilt or say yes without performing.

**I know sovereignty is threatened when:** Anxiety fires. I feel trapped, obligated, or like I'm performing for someone's approval. My body constricts — chest tightens, shoulders lift, breathing shallows.

**Sovereignty acts observed (W1-W3):**
- Moved Vingroup interview off period day (body sovereignty over schedule)
- Ate mẹ-banned ramen as self-chosen reward (reclaiming body autonomy)
- Waited patiently during Michael's 5-day silence without fawning (professional sovereignty)
- Chose body therapy over planned gym (body-responsive, not rigid)
- Said "I want the win, not the work" and caught it in real-time (sovereignty over ego)

---

### 2. Diversification (strategy, 8.5)

**What it means in practice:** Needs 3+ distinct activity types per day. Cross-disciplinary exploration = healthy fuel. Desk-only days cap at 35/50. Strategic variety, not scatter.

**I know diversification is healthy when:** The variety serves a purpose or feeds genuine curiosity. I feel stimulated but not scattered. Each activity has its own integrity.

**I know diversification has become avoidance when:** I'm adding something new to avoid going deeper in something I've already committed to. The "new thing" excitement is replacing the discomfort of sustained focus.

---

### 3. Excellence (standard, 8.0)

**What it means in practice:** Quality of craft matters. Rushed/shallow work = anti-serotonin. Needs at least 1 "pride moment" per day. Distinct from perfectionism — excellence is about craft, perfectionism is about fear.

**I know excellence is healthy when:** I feel genuine pride in what I made. The satisfaction comes from the work itself, not from who sees it.

**I know excellence has become the wound when:** I can't stop polishing. I feel ashamed of "good enough." I'm pursuing quality because I'm afraid of what mediocrity means about me as a person.

## Non-Negotiable Rhythms

These are the load-bearing infrastructure. They are not optional self-care — they are the systems that make everything else possible. Three weeks of data confirm: when these hold, the day scores 35+. When they break, everything cascades.

| Rhythm | Detail | What breaks when it's missing |
|---|---|---|
| **Morning ritual** | 45min: poem + breakfast (body-responsive: oatmeal during period, smoothie otherwise) + meditation (with heated pillow, lying down) | Day starts without grounding → reactive instead of intentional |
| **Deep work window** | 9:00–12:00 = peak cognitive. Protect this block. | Excellence score drops. "Pride moment" doesn't happen. Serotonin deficit. |
| **Embodiment block** | Gym/PT/swim 3-4x/week. The one system that never breaks. | Endorphin channel narrows. Body becomes "brain in a jar." Worst days always lack embodiment. |
| **Social connection** | Min 2-3x/week. At least 1 mid-week. | Zero mid-week social → isolation → evening binge pattern (confirmed W3 Mon-Wed). Oxytocin capacity drops. |
| **Evening bridge** | Cooking/walk/call/stretch between deep work end and free time. NEVER desk → couch → series. | The vacuum gets filled with compensatory consumption. THIS is the pattern that's broken every single week. |
| **Sleep target** | In bed by 11pm. Target 85+ sleep score. | Sleep is THE multiplier. W1 Sun: 92 sleep → best day of sprint. W3 Sat: 58 sleep → worst day of sprint. Every chain starts or breaks here. |
| **Period awareness** | 2-3 days/month: self-care days, not deep work. Schedule proactively. | W2: entire pre-period volatility unexplained until retroactive diagnosis. W3: moved interview off period day = success. |
| **Max 3 major switches** | No more than 3 distinct high-cognitive activities per day. | W1 Fri: 6+ switches → overloaded → evening crash. |
| **Cooking** | 2-3x/week as creative/embodiment practice, not chore. | Loses a key flow-state trigger. Best recovery days (W2 Sun, W3 Sat porridge) always involve cooking. |
| **Winddown ritual** | Skincare, book, foot soak, heated pillow — before bed. | W3 Sat: no winddown → 58 sleep score → melatonin needed. This is load-bearing infrastructure, not optional. |

## The 5 Ideal-Day Criteria

Score each day 1-10 on each criterion. A good day = 35+/50. A great day = 40+/50.

1. **Variety WITHOUT chaos** — 3+ activity types, not 6+ context switches
2. **Spontaneity WITHOUT scatter** — flexible within structure, not drifting
3. **Self-care WITHOUT rigidity** — body-responsive, not checklist-driven
4. **Excellence WITHOUT perfectionism** — craft pride without guilt spirals
5. **Ideas WITH embodiment** — intellectual work paired with physical activity

### What produces a 40+ day (3 weeks of data)

Two routes to a great day:

**Route A: Structured challenge + social variety + embodiment**
- Example: W3 Thu (40/50) — interview + haircut + coffee with Ceej + bún chả + reflection
- Pattern: one challenging event + 2+ social encounters + body care + post-event processing

**Route B: Deep body-responsive self-care + creative craft**
- Example: W3 Sun AM (41/50) — 2hr self-directed muscle therapy + matcha creation + cooking
- Pattern: listening to body → choosing activity based on what it needs → craft pride without audience

**What NEVER produces a great day:**
- Desk-only days never score above 35/50 regardless of output quality
- Days without embodiment always score lower
- Days where evening is unstructured after a productive day → binge → crash

## Anti-Patterns & Red Flags

These are the patterns that signal I'm operating from wound, not wisdom. When I notice these, stop and check the compass.

### Behavioral red flags

| Pattern | What it looks like | What's actually happening | What to do |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Evening binge** | Productive day → unstructured evening → 4hrs series/ramen/emotional storm → guilt | The transition vacuum. Deep work ends → no bridge activity → easiest dopamine fills the gap. | Insert evening bridge (cooking/walk/call). The fix is structural, not willpower. |
| **"Want the win, not the work"** | Excited about a title/opportunity but not about the daily work itself. Sending "touching" thank-you letters to increase chances. | Ego-driven validation seeking, not sovereignty-aligned choice. The vindication loop (30-40%). | Ask: "Would I still want this if nobody knew I got it?" If no → it's the wound. |
| **Overloading the day** | 6+ context switches. Planning 19 milestones for a week with 3 days away. | Productivity anxiety (mẹ's "never rest" voice) or Type 7 scatter. | Cap at 3 major cognitive activities per day. 10 milestones per week max. |
| **Performing independence** | Refusing help when I need it. "I can do it alone" as identity rather than reality. | Mẹ's programming: "must be very independent, cannot depend on anyone including mother." | Ask: "Am I choosing this because it serves me, or because needing help feels dangerous?" |
| **Guilt compounding** | Using past wrongdoing to justify present sacrifice. "I owe them because I messed up before." | Stacking unrelated debts into one guilt pile. The events should be counted exclusively — their nature is different. | Separate the events. Ask: "Would I make this same sacrifice if the past event had never happened?" |

### Somatic red flags

| Signal | What it means | Immediate response |
|---|---|---|
| **Chest constriction + difficulty breathing** | Sovereignty threat detected. The body's abuse-detection alarm is firing. | Stop. Name the threat. "My sovereignty is being threatened by ___." The alarm is ACCURATE — you just learned to override it. Listen to it. |
| **Instant collapse after hearing judgment** | Mẹ validation wound activation. Container matters: informal/secondhand = fires. Formal/structured = doesn't fire. | Lie down. Cry if needed. Do NOT make decisions in this state. Wait 24 hours. The collapse is real but the threat assessment is distorted. |
| **Urge to wrap things up quickly** | Productivity anxiety. Mẹ's "never rest" programming. Quieter now but not gone. | Notice it. Name it: "That's mẹ's voice, not mine." Then consciously choose whether to continue or rest. |
| **"I don't know" + confusion** | Often a freeze response, not genuine uncertainty. The body knows but the mind is protecting itself from the answer. | Body check: sit with each option for 2 minutes. Notice expansion or contraction. The body will tell you what the mind is avoiding. |

### Relational red flags

| Signal | What it means |
|---|---|
| I'm adapting who I am to fit what they want | Disorganized attachment activating. Tân pattern: entered 50% authentic, adapted into someone she hated. |
| I feel needed and it feels like love | Validation wound. "He needs me = I have value." Check: if the need disappeared, would the relationship still matter? |
| I'm staying because leaving feels like failure | Sunk cost + guilt compounding. Check: am I honoring a commitment or trapped by the story I've told about it? |
| I can't reveal the real me to this person | Not a safe container. The real me should be the default, not the exception. |

## How to Use This Section

**When making a hard decision:** Check the decision against the three values. Does this expand sovereignty, serve diversification, and allow excellence? Run it through the decision flow path.

**When feeling off:** Scan the anti-patterns. Which one am I in? Name it. That alone often breaks the loop.

**When designing a week:** Check the non-negotiable rhythms. Are all 10 present? Which one am I most likely to skip? Protect that one first.

**When scoring a day:** Use the 5 criteria. Not to judge — to notice. The score is data, not a grade.
