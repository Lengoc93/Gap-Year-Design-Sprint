3. **Self-care WITHOUT rigidity** — body-responsive, not checklist-driven
4. **Excellence WITHOUT perfectionism** — craft pride without guilt spirals
5. **Ideas WITH embodiment** — intellectual work paired with physical activity

## The Universal Day Template

Before the 6 scenarios, here's the **shared skeleton** that every schedule builds on:

```
06:30–07:15  MORNING RITUAL
             Poem reading · Breakfast (body-responsive) · Meditation (heated pillow)

07:15–08:00  BODY BLOCK (variable)
             Gym / PT / swim / walk — OR move to afternoon slot

08:00–08:30  TRANSITION
             Shower · tea · settle in · review day intention

08:30–12:00  DEEP WORK BLOCK 1 ★ (protected — peak cognitive)
             [Scenario-specific: product work / research / classes / consulting]

12:00–13:00  LUNCH + SOCIAL MICRO
             Eat away from desk · call/text someone · walk outside

13:00–15:00  DEEP WORK BLOCK 2
             [Scenario-specific — lower cognitive demand than Block 1]

15:00–15:30  BREAK
             Movement / snack / quick errand / sunlight

15:30–17:30  BLOCK 3: VARIETY BLOCK
             [Scenario-specific: meetings / reading / side project / exploration]

17:30–18:30  EVENING BRIDGE ★ (non-negotiable)
             Cooking OR walk OR call someone OR stretch
             NEVER desk → couch → series. Always an active transition.

18:30–20:00  FREE / SOCIAL
             Dinner · friend · series (1hr max, chosen, not defaulted) · reading

20:00–21:00  WIND-DOWN
             Light reading / journaling / foot soak + diary / gentle prep for tomorrow

21:00–22:00  SLEEP PREP → LIGHTS OUT by 22:30
```

**Weekly rhythm (shared):**
- **Mon-Thu:** Working days (scenario-specific)
- **Fri:** Lighter day — retro, admin, exploration, spontaneity buffer
- **Sat:** Mixed — grooming/planning + one fun/social activity
- **Sun:** Recovery — unstructured creative flow, cooking, no obligations

## How to Use This Document

### Simulation Protocol
Pick ONE scenario. Live it for 3 consecutive working days (Mon-Wed or Tue-Thu). Score each day using the 5 criteria. Compare to your W1-W3 baselines.

### Simulation Day Rules
1. Follow the scenario's daily schedule as closely as possible
2. Score the 5 criteria at end of each day (before evening wind-down)
3. Note energy levels at 3 checkpoints: post-morning ritual, post-lunch, post-evening bridge
4. Log any sovereignty threats, anxiety activations, or vindication impulses
5. Track sleep score
6. At end of 3 days: which scenario made you say "I could do this 4/7 days for a year"?

### Suggested Simulation Order
1. **Week 4:** Simulate Scenario 5 (70R/30C) — highest compass match, test the variety
2. **Week 5:** Simulate Scenario 2 (Entrepreneur) — test sovereignty + isolation
3. **Week 6:** Simulate Scenario 6 (70PM/30Biz) — test the energy split
4. If needed: Simulate Scenario 1 or 3 to confirm/deny

### The Question Each Simulation Must Answer
> "On a random Tuesday at 2pm in this life — am I energized or enduring?"
