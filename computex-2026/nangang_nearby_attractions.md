## Detailed Profiles

### 1. Nangang Software Park (Phase 1 & 2) — 472 m · 7 min walk
**Why it's for you:** The park is a live working ecosystem of 400+ tech and AI companies (ASUS, Acer, Trend Micro, etc.). As a PM in AI/fintech thinking about human-machine interaction and computer vision, this is a free-range case study: product teams, dev culture, and startup energy all in one campus.

- **What to do:** Walk the inner lanes between buildings, check the food court (popular with engineers), spot company office signs to map Taiwan's tech stack
- **Free entry** to common areas; cafes and co-working spots available
- Connects directly to the station via covered walkway

---

### 2. Shanshuilu Eco Park (山水綠生態公園) — ~0.9 km · 12 min walk
**Why it's for you:** Reclaimed from a former sanitary landfill site, now a 21-hectare wetland and meadow park. Great for morning walks that clear your head before Computex days, or decompression after booth hours. Your peak state equation includes body sovereignty — this is the venue for it.

- Wetland boardwalks, open meadows, bird watching
- No admission fee
- Best in early morning (before 9am) for solitude and light
- Connects to the Nangang mountain trail system

---

### 3. Taipei Music Center (臺北流行音樂中心) — ~1.5 km · 20 min walk
**Why it's for you:** Opened in 2021 and designed by Dutch firm Mecanoo, this performing arts complex has two buildings — a live house and a music hall. Beyond concerts, it has exhibition spaces on Taiwanese pop music history. Architecturally striking from outside; good for an evening walk before/after dinner.

- Coordinates: 25.0526°N, 121.5985°E (No. 99, Civic Blvd Sec 8)
- Check performance schedule — genres from indie to electronic to traditional
- Free to walk the outdoor plaza; ticketed shows start ~NT$500

---

### 4. Nangang Park (南港公園) — ~2 km · 25 min walk
**Why it's for you:** The district's community park with a lake, jogging trail, and morning tai chi crowd. Not Instagram-worthy — exactly the right kind of ordinary that makes a place feel real. Use this for your morning run or evening decompression during Computex week.

- Lake with rowing boats (seasonal)
- 10 min walk from Kunyang MRT Station (one stop towards Xinyi on the Blue line)
- Free, always open

---

### 5. Taipei Nangang Exhibition Center (台北南港展覽館) — ~2.1 km · 27 min walk
**Why it's for you:** This is the Computex venue — Hall 1 and Hall 2. Outside of show hours, the area around it (CPC Station, Nangang Road) has the highest density of tech industry cafes, team dinners, and business hotels in the district. If you're attending Computex June 1–5, this is ground zero.

- THSR + MRT Nangang Line station underneath (Nangang Station) for easy cross-city access
- Walk or take the MRT one stop from Nangang Software Park station (Brown Line → Blue/Red line transfer at Kunyang)

---

### 6. Muzhishan / Thumb Mountain (拇指山) — ~2.5 km to trailhead · ~35 min walk
**Why it's for you:** One of Taipei's lesser-known city hikes. The Nangang Trail loop includes Muzhishan (thumb-shaped peak), 95 Peak, and the Tiaomi Old Trail — 7.2 km total with 360° views of Taipei basin, including Taipei 101 and the port. This is where your "solo trekking with confidence" bucket list item starts, one trail at a time.

- Moderate difficulty (some steep sections)
- Best before 8am to beat heat; bring 1L water minimum
- Access from Nangang Road Section 3 (bus from station, or 35 min walk)
- No fee; trail is marked

---

### 7. Academia Sinica + Museums (中央研究院) — ~2.8 km · Bus ~15 min
**Why it's for you:** Taiwan's national research institution — the most direct proxy for the Nordic PhD research environment you simulated in Week 4. Walk the campus and notice: does this feel like the right air? Two museums are open to the public:

- **Museum of the Institute of History and Philology** — bronze vessels, oracle bones, Taiwanese archaeological finds
- **Institute of Ethnology Museum** — ethnographic collections across Asia
- Both are free; open Tue–Sun
- Transit: Bus 306, 205, or 212 from Nangang Software Park area → Academia Sinica stop
- Bonus: **National Biotechnology Research Park** (see #8 below) is directly adjacent

---

### 8. National Biotechnology Research Park (國家生技研究園區) — ~3 km · Bus ~15 min
**Why it's for you:** A government-funded research and innovation campus adjacent to Academia Sinica, focused on biotech, aging research, and precision medicine. Connects directly to your interest in "aging well / physical AI / robotics." The campus is open to walk; some institutes host public talks.

- Free to walk the grounds
- Same bus route as Academia Sinica
